### Why this project?
It started with me wanting to get into more complex pcb's. I had designed a couple of relatively simple tht pcb's for a [custom midi controller](https://www.github.com/tommie1236/simple-midi-controller) and a [dmx art-net node](https://www.github.com/tommie1236/pico-art-net-node). The process/journal for those 2 projects can be found on SoM.

And i love working in/with AV and mostly lighting equipment and the birthday of a buddy with whom i have a little AV services company was coming up. So i thought it would be a cool idea to make a tiny desk sized stage light toy for him that can be controlled via DMX512 like actual stage lights. 

### The process

**Just started with listing the requirements:**
- needs to be able to recieve actual DMX512 signals (basicly a glorified and differential uart)
- needs to control 2 small servo motors for pan and tilt.
- needs to be usb-c powered (and allow for usb-c to usb-c cables. don't need whole PD power)
- needs to control 1 (or 7, depending if i go with a moving head spot or wash, not sure yet) 10mm ws2812b leds. 
- needs to be able to have a easily settable dmx address via dip switches. 

And went searching for mcu's that didn't require many or complicated external components, were small in footprint and had enough pins. I arrived at the attiny-1 series from microchip that looked pretty good and chose the attiny1616. This would be a challenge on its own because i have pretty much only worked with rp2040 based boards before and almost no AVR mcu's. 
Probably spend around 20-30 hours researching and creating the schematic/pcb but don't know exactly as wakatime isn't working in kicad.
